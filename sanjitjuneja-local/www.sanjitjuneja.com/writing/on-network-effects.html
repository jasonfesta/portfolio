On network effects

The geometry of compounding — why most products grow by addition, why a few multiply, and what the substrate has always been doing for the second kind.

April 26, 2026 · 3 min read

Most products grow linearly: each new user adds roughly the same amount of value.

A small number of products compound. Their value to each participant increases as the network grows.

Great product strategy is often about identifying and protecting that compounding loop before scale arrives.
